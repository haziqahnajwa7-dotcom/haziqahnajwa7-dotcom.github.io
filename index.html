Mathematical Modeling

Biological growth curves follow a sigmoidal model. To calculate the instantaneous growth rate, we take the first derivative of the logistic function.

Cumulative Growth \( f(x) \):
\[f(x) = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)}}\]
Instantaneous Growth Rate \( f'(x) \):
\[f'(x) = \frac{L \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(x - x_0)}}{(1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)})^2}\]
Derivatives Explained: Standard farm records show the total weight at harvest, but the derivative \( f'(x) \) informs us of the exact grams gained per individual day. This reveals structural cell changes that simple observation cannot detect.

📚 Academic Context & Biophysical Data References

Biological Growth Curve Fitting

As verified in clinical postharvest literature across Southeast Asia, the development of high-solids tropical fruits undergoes distinct developmental milestones. In the initial 0 to 45 Days After Anthesis (DAA), growth is dominated by micro-cellular dividing within the endocarp. It is mathematically flat. Once cell division reaches capacity, the exponential cell expansion phase (45 - 85 DAA) kicks off, where the fruit behaves as a strong carbohydrate metabolic sink.

This leads to a maximum instantaneous rate of change (inflection point at 70 DAA). Understanding the exact inflection point assists Malaysian farmers in timing high-potassium (K) fertilizer applications to maximize flesh quality and avoid rind rupture.
